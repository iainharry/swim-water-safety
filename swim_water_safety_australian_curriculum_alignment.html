Context — What This Suite Is and Where It Came From

This resource suite was developed collaboratively by an HPE teacher and educational designer to address a gap in readily available, classroom-ready, dryland swimming and water safety teaching resources. The 77+ resources in the suite cover Years 7–8 and Years 9–10 and require no pool access to deliver — everything is designed for a standard classroom or gymnasium setting.

The suite was originally developed against the Victorian Curriculum F–10 Version 2.0 (VCAA), specifically the Health and Physical Education learning area and its safety focus area. It draws on resources and research from Lifesaving Victoria (LSV), Royal Life Saving Society Australia (RLSSA), Surf Life Saving Australia (SLSA), AUSTSWIM, Paddle Australia, and international bodies including RLSS UK and the CDC.

Important distinction for non-Victorian teachers The Victorian Curriculum V2.0 is VCAA's adaptation of the Australian Curriculum V9.0 — they are related but not identical. Strand names, content description codes (VC2HP8P / VC2HP8M vs AC9HP8P / AC9HP8M), and some focus area boundaries differ. This document maps the suite directly to Australian Curriculum V9.0 codes so teachers in all other states and territories can use it confidently.

The core pedagogical approach is consistent with Australian Curriculum HPE principles: a strength-based, inquiry-oriented approach to health literacy; analysis of risk and health information; and movement skill development in authentic contexts. The content is highly transferable across jurisdictions.

Australian Curriculum V9.0 — State & Territory Adoption

All Australian states and territories are required to adopt Australian Curriculum V9.0. Timelines and adaptation approaches differ. The table below gives a current-as-of-2026 summary so you can judge how directly the AC9HP codes in this document apply to your school.

Queensland
QCAA — unmodified AC
✔ AC V9 — Staged rollout 2024–2028

Queensland implements the Australian Curriculum as written, with no state adaptation. AC9HP codes used in this document apply directly. QCAA resources and standard elaborations are fully compatible.

Australian Capital Territory
ACT Education
✔ AC V9 — Implemented from 2024

ACT adopts the Australian Curriculum directly. AC9HP codes used in this document apply without modification. Full implementation required from 2024.

Tasmania
TASC / DoE Tasmania
✔ AC V9 — Implemented from 2023

Tasmania was among the earliest adopters of V9 in government schools. AC9HP codes apply directly. Some independent schools may still be in transition.

South Australia
DECD / SA Education
✔ AC V9 — All schools required from 2024

All SA school sectors (government, Catholic, independent) are required to implement AC V9.0. AC9HP codes apply directly.

Northern Territory
NT Department of Education
✔ AC V9 — Implementation underway

NT implements AC V9.0 with full adoption required. AC9HP codes apply directly. Note: NT has significant Indigenous community contexts — see localisation guidance in Section 5.

New South Wales
NESA — adopt & adapt
→ Adapting V9 into NSW syllabuses

NESA uses an 'adopt and adapt' approach — AC content is incorporated into NSW syllabuses with local additions. New HPE syllabuses aligned to V9 are in development. In the interim, check NESA PDHPE syllabuses for alignment. Core content descriptions are highly compatible.

Western Australia
SCSA — adopt & adapt
→ HPE mandated from 2025

WA has adapted V9 into the Western Australian Curriculum for HPE, mandated from 2025. The WA adaptation is closely aligned to AC V9.0 HPE content descriptions. Verify codes against SCSA's curriculum browser for exact WA code equivalents.

For NSW and WA teachers Even where your jurisdiction uses adapted syllabuses rather than direct AC codes, the content descriptions in this resource suite align to the same underlying concepts — risk assessment, protective behaviours, health information analysis, and movement in aquatic contexts. Cross-reference the AC9HP codes below with your syllabus content descriptions; the match will be evident at the concept level even if codes differ.

The vast majority of content in this suite is directly transferable across Australia. The pedagogical approach, health literacy framework, curriculum alignment, and most of the lesson content require no modification. However, a small number of elements reference Victorian-specific organisations, legislation, or statistics. The table below guides you on what to keep, what to swap, and what to check.

How to Use This Suite in Your School

Getting started — four steps

  1. Download the suite from the GitHub repository or shared drive link provided by the resource creators.
  2. Review the Unit Overview document (water_safety_unit_overview.html) — this gives you a complete map of all 77+ resources, what order they go in, and where assessment sits.
  3. Check the localisation table above (Section 5) and make any necessary swaps before distributing to students.
  4. Start with the Teacher Resource Guide — it gives you the full lesson plan with timing, differentiation, and assessment guidance built in.

Delivery models

The suite is designed for three delivery contexts — all without pool access:

  • 5-week unit (Years 7–8): One 50–60 min lesson per week. Use Student Workbook, Teacher Guide, Scenario Cards, and assessment task.
  • Full-term subject: Extend with the Advanced Workbook (Y9–10), Data Pack, Cross-Curricular Literacy Unit, and Debate Framework.
  • Single-lesson standalone: The Substitute Teacher Pack is self-contained and requires no prior unit context.

Curriculum planning documentation

When writing your unit plan, use the AC V9.0 codes from Section 3 of this document. For Queensland teachers, the QCAA standard elaborations for Years 7–8 HPE are directly compatible. For WA, cross-reference with SCSA's curriculum browser using the content description text — the WA adaptation closely mirrors the AC descriptions.

Assessment

The suite includes formative assessment (quiz question bank, workbook activities, exit tickets) and summative assessment (student action plan, portfolio template, extended response rubric). Assessment tasks can be used as-is or modified — all are criterion-referenced and include teacher rubrics.
