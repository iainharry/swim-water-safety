The 5 Water Safety Rules

🚩 Rule 1

Read the signs

Always check flags and warning signs before you swim.

🦶 Rule 2

Feet first

Never dive into water you don't know. Always enter feet first.

📏 Rule 3

Stay in your depth

Only swim where you can touch the bottom if you need to.

👫 Rule 4

Swim with a friend

Never swim alone. Always have someone with you.

🙋 Rule 5

Signal for help

If you need help, raise ONE arm up high and keep it there.

Beach Flags — What Do They Mean?

🟥🟨

Red & Yellow Flags

SWIM HERE. A lifeguard is watching. This is the safest spot. Always swim between these flags.

🟥

Single Red Flag

DANGER. Conditions are very bad. Do NOT swim here. Expert swimmers only with extreme care.

🟧

Orange Windsock

NO INFLATABLES. Strong wind is blowing out to sea. Inflatables will blow away and can't come back.

🟪

Purple Flag

MARINE DANGER. Dangerous sea creatures are in the water. Do NOT enter.

Caught in a Rip? What to Do

A rip is a fast stream of water pulling out to sea. Don't fight it! If you swim against it, you will get too tired to survive.
1
😌

Stay calm. Don't panic.

2
🛟

Float. Save your energy.

3
↔️

Swim sideways — not back to shore.

4
↗️

Angle back in when out of the rip.

5
🙋

Raise ONE arm to signal for help.

Rescuing Someone — Throw, Don't Go

✅ DO THIS

✔Call 000 first
✔Throw a rope, towel, pool noodle, or anything that floats
✔Talk to them — keep them calm
✔Pull them in using what you threw
✔Stay on the edge — don't go in

❌ DON'T DO THIS

✗Jump in after them if you're not trained
✗Panic — panicking wastes time
✗Try to grab their hand by leaning over
✗Assume someone else will help
