Keeping Your Child
Safe Near Water

Your child is currently completing our Swim & Water Safety unit in HPE. This sheet summarises the key messages so the whole family can be water safe together.

Why this matters: In 2023–24, 54 people drowned fatally in Victoria — the highest toll in over a decade. Nearly 1 in 5 victims were aged 15–24. All 54 deaths occurred at unpatrolled locations. The good news: drowning is almost entirely preventable with the right knowledge and habits.

🌊 The 5 LSV SwimSafe Rules

  • Read signs — always check flags and warnings before entering any water
  • Enter feet first — never dive into unknown water
  • Stay in depth — only swim where you can stand up
  • Swim with a friend — never swim alone
  • Signal for help — raise ONE arm if you're in trouble

🏖 Beach Flag Quick Guide

  • 🔴🟡 Red & Yellow — Patrolled zone — always swim here
  • 🔴 Red flag — Dangerous — extreme caution
  • ⬛⬜ Black & White — Surfers/boards only — no swimmers
  • 🟡⚫ Yellow & Black — Swimming only, no boards
  • 🟠 Orange windsock — Offshore winds — no inflatables
  • 🟣 Purple — Dangerous marine life present

🌊 If Caught in a Rip Current

  • Stay calm — do not panic or fight the current
  • Float — conserve energy
  • Swim parallel to shore to escape the current's channel
  • Angle back to the beach at an angle
  • Signal — raise one arm if you need help

Never swim directly against a rip — it will exhaust you.

✅ What Families Can Do

  • Always swim at patrolled beaches between the flags
  • Download the BeachSafe app (free) to find your nearest patrolled beach
  • Never let children or teens swim alone or at unpatrolled locations
  • Know where your nearest AED (defibrillator) is located
  • Talk about peer pressure — it's okay to say no to unsafe swimming dares
  • Avoid alcohol near water — it significantly increases risk
  • Consider a first aid course — St John Ambulance Vic runs family courses
000

In a water emergency, call 000 immediately

Say: your location (street, beach name, or nearest landmark), what happened, how many people are injured, and whether the person is breathing. Stay on the line — the operator will guide you. While waiting: attempt a throw rescue using a rope, towel, or any floating object. Throw, don't go.
