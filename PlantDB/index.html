Bioregions & Climate Zones

Understanding Victorian bioregions is essential for appropriate plant selection. Most of Melbourne is covered by the Gippsland Plain, Victorian Volcanic Plain, and the Highlands – Southern Fall. When a plant occurs naturally in Victoria within specific bioregions, this provides key information about its preferred growing conditions.

  • Bioregions of Victoria – VicFlora — Click individual bioregions for detailed climate and flora information

  • Artificial light
  • Damage from human activities
  • Drought stress
  • Dust impacts

  • Increased temperatures
  • Leaks from services (water, sewers, gas)
  • Maintenance regimes
  • Nutrient deficiencies / toxicities
  • Oxygen deficiency in soil

  • pH values (adverse)
  • Pruning near overhead power lines
  • Quality of initial plant stock
  • Reflections from buildings
  • Rooting space restrictions

  • Soil contamination
  • Soil disturbances and compaction
  • Temperature variation

Column Descriptions

Plant Types 1–3: Multiple classifications can apply. Type 2 = life cycle; Type 3 = origin/taxonomy.

Description: Distinguishing features, canopy type, hazardousness, weediness, and uses.

Indigenous Uses/Notes: Optional — record traditional uses and cultural significance where known and appropriate.

GPS / Map Reference: Location data for planted specimens — GPS as decimal degrees, map reference as grid notation.

Custom Tags & Notes: Freeform labels and personal observations per plant.
