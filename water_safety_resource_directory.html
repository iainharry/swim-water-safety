Life Saving Victoria (LSV)

🏊

Life Saving Victoria — Main Organisation

Peak Body · Victorian

LSV is Victoria's primary aquatic safety organisation. They deliver school programs, publish annual drowning data, provide the School Swimming Toolkit, and work in partnership with VCAA on curriculum development. Their classroom-based resources are free and curriculum-aligned. The LSV Toolkit is the single most useful starting point for Victorian HPE teachers delivering water safety.

https://lsv.com.au https://lsv.com.au/toolkit/classroom_based_water_safety.php https://lsv.com.au/research/victorian-drowning-reports/ (annual drowning reports) https://lsv.com.au/toolkit/curriculum_more.php (curriculum alignment tool)
✅ Free 💧 Fully dry-land usable 📋 VCAA-co-developed 📊 Annual drowning data

🌊 LSV SwimSafe Rules — Key Dry-Land Teaching Content

🪧

Read Signs

Always read warning signs and beach flags before entering any water environment

🧗

Enter Feet First

Never dive into unknown water — submerged objects and shallow depth cause spinal injuries

📏

Stay In Depth

Only swim in water where your feet can touch the bottom, unless you are a confident swimmer

👫

Swim With a Friend

Never swim alone — always have someone with you who can call for help

🙋

Signal For Help

Raise one arm and call out if you need assistance. Stay calm and float to conserve energy

📊

LSV Annual Drowning Reports (2023–24 Key Data)

Research Data · Annual

LSV publishes a detailed annual drowning report based on State Coroner data. The 2023–24 report showed 54 fatal drownings in Victoria — the highest in over a decade. Nearly 1 in 5 victims were aged 15–24, and ALL deaths occurred at unpatrolled locations. This data is powerful for classroom discussion and numeracy tasks.

https://lsv.com.au/research/victorian-drowning-reports/ https://blog.lsv.com.au/2025/07/09/swimming-and-water-safety-in-the-new-victorian-curriculum-f-10-2-0/
✅ Free / Public 📊 Numeracy cross-curriculum 💧 Dry-land data activity

🏅

Royal Life Saving Society Australia — Swim and Survive

National Organisation

RLSSA's Swim and Survive program is Australia's national learn-to-swim and water safety framework. It includes dry-land classroom modules that can be taught independently of pool sessions. The Bronze e-Lifesaving program (secondary level) addresses risk-taking, peer pressure, alcohol around water, and emergency response — ideal for Year 7–8.

Best-Practice Dry-Land Teaching Approaches

🎭

Scenario-Based Learning

Present real or realistic drowning scenarios in Victorian contexts. Students apply knowledge to decide the safest course of action.

  • River after rain
  • Unpatrolled beach on New Year's Day
  • Pool party — unresponsive person
  • Rope swing into dam
🙌

Role Play & Simulation

Students physically simulate rescue actions in the classroom — no water needed.

  • Throw rescue with towels/ropes
  • Calling 000 role-play
  • DRS ABCD sequencing on mats
  • Peer pressure decision scenarios
🗺

Hazard Mapping

Students analyse images or maps of waterways, identifying and ranking hazards by risk level.

  • Annotated beach images
  • River cross-section diagrams
  • Pool environment analysis
  • Parks Victoria location maps
📊

Data Analysis (Cross-Curricular)

Use LSV drowning data for numeracy activities — percentages, graphs, trends over time.

  • Calculate percentage by age group
  • Graph 10-year trends
  • Compare beach vs inland rates
  • Compare Australian vs global data
⚖️

Decision-Making Models

Teach frameworks that students can apply to any water safety situation:

  • LSV SwimSafe rules
  • RLSSA risk assessment framework
  • Red Cross "Think so you don't sink"
  • STOP–THINK–ACT structure
🎨

Communication Tasks

Students create water safety materials for real audiences — develops literacy, ICT, and health promotion skills.

  • Social media post for teens
  • Poster for new migrants (CALD)
  • Letter to a younger sibling
  • Script for a 30-second radio ad

Australian Resuscitation Council (ARC)

First Aid · CPR Evidence · Clinical Guidelines

Australia's peak body for resuscitation guidelines and clinical evidence. The ARC publishes evidence-based resuscitation guidelines that underpin the CPR content taught throughout this suite — including compression rate (100–120 bpm), depth (5–6cm), ratio (30:2), and the physiological rationale for bystander CPR within 4 minutes. Referenced in the Year 9–10 Extension Workbook as a primary source for the CPR evidence evaluation task (Q2.2) and in the Teacher Professional Learning Notes.

🩺 CPR clinical guidelines 📊 Bystander CPR evidence 🏥 Primary source for Q2.2
https://www.resus.org.au/guidelines
❤️

Heart Foundation Australia

Cardiac Health · CPR Education · Public Health

The National Heart Foundation of Australia funds cardiac research, advocates for heart health policy, and provides public education on CPR and heart health. Cardiac physiology and CPR rationale referenced in this suite — particularly the Year 9–10 physiology sections explaining cardiac output, heart function, and the physiological basis for CPR — is informed by Heart Foundation evidence. The Heart Foundation's community CPR awareness campaigns complement the water safety CPR content taught in this unit.

💓 Cardiac output explanation 🏥 CPR physiology 📚 Year 9–10 physiology section
https://www.heartfoundation.org.au
🏛️

Consumer Affairs Victoria

Victorian Government · Pool Fencing Legislation · Consumer Protection

The Victorian Government agency responsible for administering consumer protection laws, including residential swimming pool and spa fencing legislation. Victorian legislation mandates four-sided isolation pool fencing for all new residential pools — a requirement with strong evidence for reducing child drowning. Referenced in the Year 9–10 Extension Workbook Q3.2 (policy critique activity) and in the Year 9–10 advanced workbook prevention strategy evaluation section.

⚖️ Pool fencing legislation 🏊 Home pool safety 📋 Year 9–10 Extension Q3.2
https://www.consumer.vic.gov.au/housing/swimming-pools-and-spas/fencing
🌐

World Bank — Open Data (Country Income Classifications)

International · Development Data · Country Classification

The World Bank Open Data platform provides country income classifications (High / Upper-middle / Lower-middle / Low income) used in the Year 9–10 Extension Workbook Q1.3 (international statistical comparison activity). Students are asked to classify comparison countries by income category to contextualise drowning rate differences — recognising that 90% of global drowning deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries (WHO). World Bank data is licensed CC BY 4.0 and freely available.

📊 Income classification data 🌍 International comparison 📋 Year 9–10 Extension Q1.3
https://data.worldbank.org

Surf Life Saving Australia (SLSA)

Water Safety · Beach Patrol · BeachSafe App

Australia's peak coastal water safety organisation, coordinating the volunteer lifesaver network at Australian beaches and managing the BeachSafe app and website (beachsafe.org.au).
