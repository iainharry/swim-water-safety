How to use this document: This outline shows you what you will be learning, doing, and achieving across the five lessons of our Water Safety unit. At the start of each lesson, read the Learning Intention (what we're aiming for) and the Success Criteria (what you can do to show you've got it). At the end of each lesson, check the criteria — can you tick them? If not, that's what to focus on before the next lesson.
📋 Unit Overview — What this unit is about
Unit Title Swim & Water Safety: Making Smart Choices in Victorian Waters
Year Level Year 7 (Ages 12–13)
Duration 5 × 50-minute lessons
Big Question "What knowledge, skills and decisions could prevent most Victorian drowning deaths — including among people our age?"
Why it matters In 2023–24, 54 people drowned in Victoria. Nearly 1 in 5 were aged 15–24. All deaths occurred at unpatrolled locations. This unit gives you the knowledge and skills to protect yourself and help others.
Resources you'll use Student Workbook · Poster Pack (4 posters) · Scenario Activity Cards · BeachSafe app (beachsafe.org.au) · Life Saving Victoria data (lsv.com.au)
Assessment Workbook (Sections 1–6, collected end of unit) and Communication Campaign task (Lesson 5) — design a water safety product for a real audience of your choice

Lesson 1

Victoria's Drowning Crisis — Why It Matters to You

⏱ 50 min 📖 Workbook Section 1 🏫 Classroom VCHPEP126 VCHPEP129
🎯 Learning Intention

We are learning to understand why water safety matters in Victoria by investigating current drowning data and explaining the key risk factors that affect young people.

✅ Success Criteria — I can show I've achieved this when I can:
Identify and interpret at least four key facts from the LSV 2023–24 Victorian drowning report (e.g. total deaths, age groups, locations, gender) and explain what each fact tells us about risk
VCHPEP129
Explain at least two reasons why teenagers and young people in Victoria face a higher drowning risk, drawing on the data and connecting it to real-life behaviours such as peer pressure and overconfidence
VCHPEP126
Describe why swimming at patrolled locations is the single most important action to reduce drowning risk, using evidence from the Victorian data to support the statement (e.g. zero deaths at patrolled locations in 2023–24)
VCHPEP126

Lesson 2

Beach Flags, Signals & Rip Current Survival

⏱ 50 min 📖 Workbook Section 2 🏫 Classroom VCHPEP126
🎯 Learning Intention

We are learning to identify Australian beach flag signals and apply the correct survival response to a rip current situation.

✅ Success Criteria — I can show I've achieved this when I can:
Correctly name and describe the purpose of all six beach flags used in Australia (red & yellow, red, black & white quartered, yellow + black ball, orange windsock, purple), including where swimmers should and should not go
VCHPEP126
Describe in sequence the five survival steps to take if caught in a rip current — Stay calm → Float → Swim parallel → Angle back to shore → Signal for help — and explain why swimming directly against the current is dangerous
VCHPEP126
Analyse a scenario (Liam's story) and identify at least two mistakes made, explaining what the safer course of action would have been using correct water safety vocabulary
VCHPEP126

Lesson 3

Emergency Response — DRS ABCD & Rescue Skills

⏱ 50 min 📖 Workbook Section 3 🏫 Classroom + Practical VCHPEP126 VCHPEP130 VCHPEM146
🎯 Learning Intention

We are learning to apply the DRS ABCD emergency action plan in a water-related scenario and demonstrate safe rescue principles without entering the water.

✅ Success Criteria — I can show I've achieved this when I can:
Recall and correctly sequence all seven DRS ABCD steps from memory — Danger, Response, Send (call 000), Airway, Breathing, CPR, Defibrillation — and describe the specific action required at each step
VCHPEP126
Explain and apply the 'Throw, Don't Go' principle, including why untrained bystanders who enter the water to help often drown themselves, and identify at least three objects that can be used as throw rescue aids
VCHPEP130
Demonstrate the correct body position and rhythm for chest compressions during CPR practice (30 compressions: 2 breaths cycle, approximately 100–120 bpm), and describe what information to give when calling 000 in a water emergency
VCHPEM146
📋 What we'll do this lesson
🔥

🔍
Explore
15 min

CPR demonstration by teacher, then student practice in pairs on cushions/mats. 30 compressions + 2 breaths

📣
Explain
8 min

Throw rescue technique — demonstrated with rope/towel. RLSSA hierarchy: Talk → Reach → Throw → Row → Go

✏️
Elaborate
15 min

Lesson 4

Hazard Mapping, Safe Decisions, Inland Waterways & Alcohol

⏱ 50 min 📖 Workbook Sections 4–5 🏫 Classroom VCHPEP125 VCHPEP126
🎯 Learning Intention

We are learning to identify and rank hazards across different Victorian water environments, evaluate the impact of peer pressure and alcohol on water safety decisions, and apply the LSV SwimSafe rules to plan for a safe water experience.

✅ Success Criteria — I can show I've achieved this when I can:
Identify and rank specific hazards in at least three Victorian water environments (beach, river/dam, pool), explain why rivers and dams cause the most Victorian drowning deaths, and link this to evidence from LSV data
VCHPEP126
Recall all five LSV SwimSafe rules from memory and apply them to a realistic personal water safety scenario — demonstrating that the rules can be used to make safer choices in real Victorian settings
VCHPEP126
Describe at least two specific strategies to resist peer pressure to swim somewhere unsafe, and explain at least three ways alcohol increases the risk of drowning — connecting both to the patterns seen in Victorian drowning data
VCHPEP125
📋 What we'll do this lesson
🔥
Engage
8 min

"Would You?" — values clarification. Stand left (would) or right (wouldn't) for three water risk scenarios. Discuss why

🔍
Explore
15 min

Activity 4.0: Hazard Mapping in pairs — list and rank hazards at beach, river/dam, and pool. Apply SwimSafe rules using Poster 4

📣
Explain
10 min

Cold water shock in Victorian inland waterways — the 1-10-1 rule, Alpine lake temperatures, Paddle Australia safety resources

✏️
Elaborate
12 min

Activities 4.1 (Risk vs. Benefit), 5.2 (Throw Rescue ranking), 5.3 (Alcohol & Water). Scenario Cards D1 and D2 as extensions

📝
Evaluate
5 min

Share with a partner: which SwimSafe rule is most important for you personally and why?

📚 Key Vocabulary
cold water shock gasp reflex inland waterway SwimSafe rules hazard ranking peer pressure risk vs. benefit 1-10-1 rule

Lesson 5

Personal Action Plan & Communication Campaign

⏱ 50 min 📖 Workbook Section 6 🏫 Classroom 📝 Assessment VCHPEP129 VCHPEP130
🎯 Learning Intention

We are learning to apply our water safety knowledge by creating a personal action plan for a real Victorian setting and producing a communication product that promotes water safety for a specific audience.

✅ Success Criteria — I can show I've achieved this when I can:
Produce a personalised water safety action plan for a chosen Victorian water activity (beach, river, pool, or paddling), identifying specific hazards, safety strategies, emergency plan, and equipment — going beyond generic answers to show real personal thinking
VCHPEP130
Create a water safety communication product (social media post, letter, or community fact sheet) that clearly targets a specific audience, includes at least one accurate statistic with its source URL, and includes a clear call to action
VCHPEP130
Select and use credible health information sources — such as Life Saving Victoria, Paddle Australia, Parks Victoria, or Royal Life Saving Society Australia — to support the claims made in the communication campaign, correctly citing the source URL
VCHPEP129
📋 What we'll do this lesson
🔥
Engage

Revision quiz (Kahoot/Quizlet) covering all 4 previous lessons. Optional: Scenario Card rapid-fire showdown as a class

✏️
Elaborate
30 min

📝

📋 Assessment Task — Communication Campaign Options
Option A

Social media post (Instagram/TikTok style) for teenagers. 1–2 slides. Must include a statistic + source + call to action.

Option B

Letter (~200 words) to a Year 5 student explaining three things they must know about Victorian water safety before summer.

Option C ★

Multilingual community fact sheet about beach flags and Victorian waterway dangers for someone new to Australia. 5 key points + 2 statistics.
