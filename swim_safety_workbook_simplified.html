Why Water Safety Matters in Victoria

📋 Key Facts — Remember These!

💧
54 people drowned in Victoria in 2023–24. That is a LOT of people.
👦
1 in 5 people who drowned were aged 15–24 years old — the same age as you in a few years.
🚫
ZERO deaths happened at patrolled beaches or supervised pools. Every single death happened somewhere without a lifeguard.
👨
Males are 4 times more likely to drown than females.
🏞
Most drownings happen at rivers, dams and lakes — not at the beach.

Beach Flags and Rip Currents

📋 Key Facts — Beach Flags

🔴🟡
Red and Yellow flags = ALWAYS swim here. A lifeguard is watching you.
🔴
Single red flag = Dangerous. Only very experienced swimmers should enter.
⬛⬜
Black and white squares = For surfboards only. Swimmers must stay out.
🟡⚫
Yellow with black ball = Swimmers only. No surfboards.
🟠
Orange windsock = Offshore winds. NO inflatable toys — they will blow out to sea.
🟣
Purple flag = Dangerous sea creatures (e.g. jellyfish). Be careful.

Emergency Response — DRS ABCD

📋 DRS ABCD — What to Do in an Emergency

🔍
D = Danger — Check for danger FIRST. Is it safe for YOU to go near?
👋
R = Response — Tap their shoulder and call their name loudly.
📞
S = Send for help — Call 000 (or tell someone else to call).
👄
A = Airway — Tilt their head back gently to open their airway.
👂
B = Breathing — Look, listen and feel — are they breathing?
💓
C = CPR — 30 chest pushes, then 2 breaths. Keep going until help arrives.
⚡
D = Defibrillation — Use the AED machine if one is nearby. It tells you exactly what to do.

⚠️ THROW, DON'T GO — The Most Important Rule!

If someone is drowning, DO NOT jump in unless you are trained. You might drown too! Instead: throw them a rope, towel, pool noodle, or anything that floats. Call 000 first.

Making Safe Decisions Near Water

📋 The 5 LSV SwimSafe Rules

🪧
Read signs — Always check flags and warning signs before going in the water.
🦶
Enter feet first — Never dive head-first into water you don't know. There might be rocks underneath.
📏
Stay in depth — Only swim in water where your feet can touch the bottom (unless you're a strong swimmer).
👫
Swim with a friend — NEVER swim alone. If you get in trouble, no-one will know.
🙋
Signal for help — Raise ONE arm above your head if you need help.

Rivers, Dams and Cold Water

📋 Cold Water Facts

🌡
Victorian lakes and rivers can be very cold even in summer — sometimes as cold as 12°C!
😮
Cold water makes your body gasp — you might accidentally breathe in water.
💪
Cold water quickly makes your muscles stop working properly — even strong swimmers can get into trouble.
✅
The safe way to enter cold water: go in slowly, one step at a time. Never jump straight in.

Activity 5.2 — Diving and Spinal Injury: What You Need to Know

~10 min

⚠ Important Facts About Diving

  • Unsafe diving causes 10% of all spinal cord injuries in Australia
  • The most common victims are males aged 10–14 — that could be someone you know
  • Two-thirds of people injured this way are permanently disabled — often unable to move their arms or legs
  • A sandbank can form overnight — a place that was safe last week might not be safe today
  • The rule: Feet First, First Time, Every Time — always go in feet-first before diving anywhere

Three Real Stories — What Happened and Why

Story 1 — Terrigal Wharf, NSW (2025): A teenage boy dived from a wharf and hit the shallow water below. He didn't know how shallow it was. He needed a helicopter to hospital.

Story 2 — Torquay Beach, VIC (2025): A teenage boy dived at a surf beach and seriously injured his spine. A sandbank had shifted and the water was much shallower than he expected.

Story 3 — Bawley Beach, NSW (2024): A young man dived from a jetty he had jumped from before — safely. But a sandbank had formed since his last visit. He was seriously hurt.
