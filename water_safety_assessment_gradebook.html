Assessment Rubric — Water Safety Unit

📐 Assessment Tasks Overview

This rubric applies to the two summative assessment tasks in the unit:

  • Task 1 (40%): Student Workbook — completed across all 5 lessons, collected end of unit
  • Task 2 (60%): Water Safety Communication Campaign — social media post, letter, or fact sheet

Note: Adjust weighting to suit your school's reporting requirements. Formative data (exit tickets, role-play observations) informs teacher feedback but is not formally graded.

Hazard Identification
(VC2HP8P10)		 Names one or two hazards with limited accuracy; relies on general or obvious examples only Names several hazards; some accurate; minimal explanation of why each is dangerous Accurately identifies key hazards in given environments; provides brief explanation for most Accurately identifies multiple hazards across different environments; explains each clearly with reference to context Systematically identifies and ranks hazards by risk level; explains specific mechanisms of danger with reference to evidence (e.g. Victorian data or physiological effects)
Water Safety Knowledge
(VC2HP8P10)		 Demonstrates minimal knowledge; responses are vague or inaccurate; unable to describe beach flags or DRS ABCD correctly Demonstrates basic knowledge of some safety rules; some errors in DRS ABCD or flag identification Demonstrates sound knowledge of beach flags, DRS ABCD, and SwimSafe rules; mostly accurate Demonstrates thorough knowledge; accurately applies safety knowledge to different environments (beach, river, pool) Demonstrates comprehensive, accurate knowledge; connects knowledge to broader prevention principles and Victorian context; uses specific terminology correctly
Application to Scenarios
(VC2HP8P08, VC2HP8P10)		 Responses are superficial or incorrect; does not apply safety knowledge appropriately to scenarios Applies some correct steps to scenarios; significant gaps in logical reasoning; some unsafe decisions remain unjustified Applies correct safety procedures to most scenarios; reasoning is generally sound; some gaps Consistently applies correct safety procedures; justifies decisions clearly; identifies multiple risk factors per scenario Applies and justifies safety decisions with precision; anticipates secondary risks; proposes nuanced solutions; shows genuine critical thinking about tradeoffs
Decision-Making & Peer Pressure
(VC2HP8P08)		 Does not identify peer pressure as a factor; unable to propose a strategy for resisting unsafe decisions Identifies peer pressure as a factor; proposes a basic strategy with limited detail or realistic application Explains how peer pressure influences water safety decisions; describes a realistic strategy for managing peer pressure Analyses peer pressure in context; proposes specific, realistic strategies with clear justification; connects to personal experience Demonstrates sophisticated understanding of social factors influencing risk decisions near water; proposes multiple strategies; considers cultural and gender factors with evidence support
Emergency Response
(VC2HP8P10)		 Unable to describe DRS ABCD correctly; does not understand throw rescue principle Describes some DRS ABCD steps; errors or omissions in sequence; partial understanding of rescue safety Describes DRS ABCD in correct order with reasonable accuracy; understands "throw, don't go" principle Accurately describes and applies DRS ABCD in scenario contexts; explains throw rescue with specifics; understands when to call 000 Demonstrates thorough mastery of emergency response; applies DRS ABCD to complex scenarios; articulates rationale for each step; understands CPR physiology and AED use
Communication Campaign Task
(VC2HP8P10)		 Incomplete or very brief; message is unclear; audience not considered; no accurate data used Attempts an appropriate format; message is mostly clear; some relevant safety information; limited use of data Clear message appropriate for chosen audience; includes at least one accurate statistic; communicates effectively Compelling, well-structured message clearly targeting chosen audience; accurate statistics used effectively; includes a clear call to action Professional-quality communication; audience-specific language and design; multiple accurate statistics with source attribution; persuasive and evidence-based call to action
Personal Action Plan
(VC2HP8P10)		 Generic or incomplete plan; "I will be careful" type responses; lacks specificity Some personal commitment evident; at least one specific safety rule identified; limited connection to own life Specific, realistic commitments to personal water safety; shows connection between knowledge and own behaviour Thoughtful, personalised action plan; multiple specific commitments; shows genuine reflection on own habits and risk contexts Comprehensive action plan reflecting sophisticated self-awareness; considers both personal and community responsibility; demonstrates transfer of learning to real life

📈 Achievement Standard Links — Level 7–8

By the end of Level 8, students are expected to:

  • Investigate strategies that enhance their own and others' health, safety and wellbeing
  • Justify actions that promote their own and others' health, safety and wellbeing at home, school and in the community
  • Gather and analyse health information to make informed decisions
  • Explain personal and social skills required to maintain respectful relationships
  • Demonstrate control and accuracy when performing specialised movement skills

📊 Grade Conversion Guide

A — Extending: Student demonstrates outstanding depth of understanding, analysis, and application beyond expected level
B — Building: Student demonstrates strong understanding and applies knowledge confidently across most tasks
C — Consolidating: Student demonstrates sound, accurate understanding appropriate to the year level standard
D — Developing: Student demonstrates partial understanding; some key knowledge or skills not yet secure
E — Emerging: Student demonstrates limited understanding; requires significant additional support
