Persuasion, Purpose & Audience — How Health Campaigns Work

⏱ 50 minVC2HP10P09 · VC2E9LY05 · VC2E9LA03Individual + Class

Before you can create an effective water safety campaign, you need to understand how health campaigns work and why some succeed while others fail.

The AAPPC Framework — Use This for Every Campaign You Analyse

A — Audience

Who is this campaign targeting? How do you know? What assumptions does it make about its audience?

A — Aim / Purpose

What does the campaign want the audience to KNOW, THINK, or DO? Which of these is primary?

P — Persuasion Technique

Fear appeal? Social proof? Reframing? Authority? Emotional appeal? Narrative? Evidence?

P — Platform / Form

What medium is it using? Social media, poster, video, radio? Why this medium for this audience?

C — Credibility

Who produced it? What evidence or authority backs the message? Is it cited?

Critique

What does it do well? What limitation or gap does it have? Who might it NOT reach?

Visual Language & Multimodal Design

⏱ 50 minVC2E9LY08 · VC2HP10P08Individual

Effective health communication uses visual, linguistic, and spatial elements together — this is called multimodal communication. Understanding how these elements work gives you more power as both a consumer and creator of health messaging.

Visual Language Toolkit — Apply to Campaigns You Analyse

Colour psychology

Blue = trust, calm, water. Red = danger, urgency. Green = safety, go. Yellow = caution, energy. How do campaigns use colour to prime an emotional response before reading a word?

Typography

Font weight (bold = authority), font size (hierarchy of importance), font style (serif = tradition/trust, sans-serif = modern/accessible). Capitalisation for urgency.

Image selection

Who is shown? Who is NOT shown? What activities and emotions are depicted? Whose perspective is centred? Who is the victim vs the hero?

Spatial composition

Where is the eye led? What is given most visual weight? What is small or marginalised? What is the call to action and where is it placed?

🎯 Assessment Summary — Cross-Curricular Unit

HPE Assessment Component

Evidence that the student can identify and critique the accessibility and effectiveness of community support services and health strategies (VC2HP10P08). Assessed through: campaign brief, target audience analysis, use of Victorian data, social determinant connection, and justification.

English / Media Component

Evidence that the student can analyse text structures and language features (AAPPC analysis), evaluate source credibility, and create a sustained, purposeful text for a specific audience. Assessed through: campaign analyses, visual analysis, and final product + justification.

A-Standard Indicators

Sophisticated AAPPC analysis that goes beyond description to evaluation. Campaign brief shows nuanced audience understanding. Justification explicitly connects persuasion theory to social determinant evidence. Final product is genuinely tailored to a specific at-risk group, not a generic audience.
