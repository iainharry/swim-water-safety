🎤 Who Might Be Visiting?

🏄

LSV / SLS Educator

Life Saving Victoria or Surf Life Saving. Expert on beach safety, drowning prevention programs, Victorian data.

🚑

Paramedic / First Responder

Frontline experience with drowning incidents. Expert on CPR, emergency response, and what happens at real incidents.

🔬

Water Safety Researcher

RLSSA, university, or government researcher. Expert on evidence, data analysis, prevention program evaluation.

👮

Water Police / Coast Guard

Expert on maritime safety, river incidents, rescue operations, and legal requirements for watercraft.

🧑‍🏫

Community Swim Educator

Runs swim programs for CALD communities, schools, or disadvantaged groups. Expert on access and equity.

💊

Emergency Physician / Nurse

Hospital perspective on near-drowning and drowning cases. Expert on physiology, CPR outcomes, secondary drowning.

About the Data & Evidence

The LSV data shows all 54 Victorian drowning deaths in 2023–24 occurred at unpatrolled locations. What do you think is the single biggest barrier to getting people to swim at patrolled locations?Data
CALD communities are over-represented in Victorian drowning deaths at approximately 1.43:1. What structural factors do you see most clearly in your work?Equity
Are there any prevention strategies you've seen that the data says should work but that don't in practice? Why do you think that is?Prevention

About the Physiology & Real Incidents

What is the most common mistake you see people make in a water emergency?Emergency

✅ During the Visit — Tips

  • Write down KEY ideas, statistics, and stories — not every word. Focus on what's new or surprising.
  • Note anything that confirms, challenges, or adds nuance to what you learned in the workbook.
  • When you ask a question, write down the answer immediately — don't rely on memory.
  • Note any specific sources, reports, or websites the speaker mentions.

📌 For Teacher Use

This tab contains guidance for teachers arranging a guest speaker or excursion for the Year 9–10 Advanced Water Safety unit.

🎤 Suggested Speakers & Contact Organisations

  • Life Saving Victoria (LSV): lsv.com.au — contact the community engagement team for school presentations. LSV educators can present on Victorian drowning data, CALD community programs, and prevention strategies.
  • Surf Life Saving Victoria: surflifesaving.com.au/vic — club educators available for school visits and career pathway presentations.
  • Royal Life Saving Society Australia: royallifesaving.com.au — research and education staff available for advanced content presentations.
  • Local Ambulance Victoria: ambulance.vic.gov.au — paramedics available through community education programs. Excellent for physiology and CPR content.
  • Water Police (Victoria Police): police.vic.gov.au — water police educators available for inland waterway and boating safety content.
  • AUSTSWIM: austswim.com.au — swim educators available for practical and career pathway content.

📋 Briefing Your Speaker

Send the following brief to your speaker before the visit:

"We are a Year 9–10 HPE class completing the Advanced Water Safety unit under the Victorian Curriculum 2.0. Students have already studied: Victorian and global drowning statistics (including CALD over-representation data), physiology of drowning and CPR mechanics, social determinants of drowning risk, evidence-based prevention strategies, and international comparisons (RLSSA, RLSS UK, Red Cross). They are preparing an evidence-based assessment task (policy brief, campaign, or school audit). The visit will be most valuable if you can share real-world context, professional experience, and career pathways, and engage with students' pre-prepared questions."

✅ Preparation Checklist

  • ☐ Speaker confirmed, date/time locked in
  • ☐ Speaker briefed on student prior knowledge and unit context
  • ☐ Students have completed "Before the Visit" tab (check completion)
  • ☐ Students have selected 3–4 questions from the question bank
  • ☐ Venue organised — space for Q&A discussion
  • ☐ "After the Visit" reflection assigned as homework (due within 24 hours)
  • ☐ Thank-you follow-up organised (student messages collected and sent)
