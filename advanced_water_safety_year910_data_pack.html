CALD Over-Representation — Structured Argument Analysis

⏱ ~15 min VC2HP10P02 Individual / Pairs Source: lsv.com.au

Use the data below to analyse CALD community over-representation in Victorian drowning deaths. You will calculate the over-representation ratio, interpret it, and construct a structured argument.

Group% of Victorian Drowning Deaths 2023–24% of Victorian Population (approx.)Ratio (deaths % ÷ population %)
CALD community members~40%~28%______
Non-CALD Victorians~60%~72%______
Worked example — Over-representation ratio:
Ratio = deaths % ÷ population % = 40 ÷ 28 = 1.43
Interpretation: CALD community members are drowning at 1.43 times the rate you would expect based on their population share. A ratio of 1.0 would mean exactly proportional representation.

Prevention context: The core Australian response to this data is the "Feet First, First Time" message — always enter unknown water feet-first, and check depth on every visit regardless of prior experience. Inland waterway locations (rivers, dams, lakes) carry the highest drowning risk of any environment in Australia and present additional hazards beyond diving injury: cold water shock, poor visibility, and remote rescue delay.
Q4.1 — Data Comparison 3 marks

The Victorian drowning rate for 2023–24 was 0.78 per 100,000 population. Diving spinal injuries affect a significantly different population profile. Compare the demographic distribution of Victorian drowning victims with the demographic distribution of aquatic spinal injury patients. What does this comparison reveal about the limitations of using drowning data alone to understand the full scope of dangerous water behaviour among young Australians?

Q4.2 — Rate Calculation Challenge 3 marks

The data shows 95% of aquatic spinal injury patients are male. If we assume the overall aquatic spinal injury population is evenly distributed across the age 10–30 male population in Australia (approximately 3.2 million), and that there are approximately 100 new cases per year nationally: (a) Calculate the rate per 100,000 for males aged 10–30. (b) How does this compare to the Victorian drowning rate (0.78 per 100,000 all ages)? (c) What are the significant limitations of this calculation?

Q4.3 — Causal Mechanism 4 marks

The post-COVID data shows a 34% increase in drowning among 15–20 year olds. Propose and evaluate THREE possible explanations for this increase. For each explanation, identify: (a) what evidence would support it, and (b) what prevention response would follow if it were the primary cause.
