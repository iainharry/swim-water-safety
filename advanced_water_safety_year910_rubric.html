How to use this rubric Read each criterion row carefully. Before submitting your work, complete the Student Self-Assessment column honestly — circle the grade descriptor that best matches your work. Your teacher will independently complete their assessment. Differences between your self-assessment and teacher assessment are a great starting point for feedback conversations.
What the grades mean A — Extending: Sophisticated, evidence-rich, insightful analysis.
B — Building: Accurate, well-supported, clearly argued.
C — Consolidating: Sound understanding, reasonable evidence.
D — Developing: Partial understanding, limited evidence.
E — Emerging: Beginning to engage with the concepts.
Grade scale: E — Emerging D — Developing C — Consolidating B — Building A — Extending

Analyses multiple determinants with specific evidence; distinguishes structural from individual causes; proposes targeted strategies linked to specific mechanisms. Sophisticated structural analysis; clearly distinguishes individual vs systemic causes; proposes evidence-based, equitable interventions; acknowledges complexity and intersectionality. 6. Diving & Spinal Injury AnalysisVC2HP10P02 · VC2HP10P08 Cannot identify the mechanism of diving-related spinal injury; confuses swimming ability with injury prevention.
