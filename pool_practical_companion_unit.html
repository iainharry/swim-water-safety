Pool-based practical companion to the Year 7–8 Swim & Water Safety dry-land unit. Run this as 3–4 pool sessions alongside or after the classroom theory unit. Aligned to VC2HP8M01.

Year 7–8 HPE 3–4 Pool Sessions × 50 min VC2HP8M01 Survival Swimming Teacher Guide + Student Checklist
Unit Overview

📋 Curriculum Alignment

This unit directly addresses VC2HP8M01: "Refine and perform specialised movement skills in aquatic environments, and apply movement concepts and strategies." It is the practical complement to the classroom theory unit covering VC2HP8P08, 126, 129, 130.

Together, the classroom theory unit and this pool unit provide complete coverage of Victorian Curriculum 2.0 HPE water safety requirements for Year 7–8.

🎯 Learning Intentions

  • Demonstrate competent survival floating technique
  • Enter water safely (feet-first from different heights)
  • Tread water for a sustained period
  • Swim a minimum distance using an efficient stroke
  • Perform a simulated throw rescue from poolside
  • Demonstrate survival back float in clothing

✅ Success Criteria

  • Can float on their back for 60+ seconds unassisted
  • Can tread water for 2+ minutes
  • Can swim 50m continuously (any stroke)
  • Can perform a correct feet-first pool entry
  • Can perform a throw rescue to a target
  • Can float in clothes for 30+ seconds

⚠️ Safety Requirements — Must Be Met Before Any Pool Session

  • Qualified lifeguard must be on deck for the entire session
  • Teacher must hold a current first aid qualification
  • All student medical forms reviewed — identify non-swimmers, cardiac conditions, asthma, epilepsy
  • Non-swimmers must be clearly identified (coloured caps recommended) and supervised in shallow end only
  • Maximum pool deck to student ratio per DE policy — confirm before the session
  • AED location confirmed and accessible
  • Students briefed on pool rules and emergency signal before entering water
  • Reference: DE Swimming and Water Safety Education Policy ↗
Lesson Plans — 4 Pool Sessions
S1

Water Entry, Survival Float & Treading Water

⏱ 50 min🏊 Pool Skills: entry, float, treadVC2HP8M01
🔥 ENGAGE 8 min

Cold Water Connection + Safety Briefing

  • Refer students back to the cold water shock content from the classroom unit: "Remember the 1-10-1 rule? Today we start practising what you'd do in that first 10 minutes."
  • Deliver pool safety briefing (use the Swimming Carnival Safety Briefing — Student sheet).
  • Identify non-swimmers; assign buddy pairs.
🏊 SKILL PRACTICE 32 min

Skills in Sequence

  • Feet-first entry (10 min): Practise entering the water feet-first from poolside (sitting, then standing with support). Reinforce SwimSafe "Enter Feet First" rule — why we never dive into unknown water.
  • Survival float — face down (10 min): Glide face-down, arms and legs relaxed and spread. Lift head to breathe when needed. Hold for progressively longer periods.
  • Back float (12 min): Arms out, hips up, ears in water, eyes up. Aim for 30 sec, then 60 sec, then 90 sec unassisted. This is the primary survival skill — if tired or in trouble, roll to back and float.
❄️ COOL DOWN + DEBRIEF 10 min

Exit the Pool + Reflection

  • Students exit safely and warm up.
  • Debrief: "Which skill felt hardest? Why is survival floating the most important skill for someone who is exhausted in open water?"
  • Students complete Session 1 rows in their Skills Checklist (self-assessment).
S2

Treading Water, Distance Swimming & Survival Stroke

⏱ 50 min🏊 Pool Skills: tread, endurance, stroke efficiency
🔥 ENGAGE 5 min

Rip Current Connection

  • Remind students: "If you're in a rip, you have about 10 minutes of useful swimming. Today we test how long you can keep moving and what stroke conserves the most energy."
🏊 SKILL PRACTICE 35 min

Skills in Sequence

  • Treading water (15 min): Eggbeater kick or breaststroke kick, arms sculling. Progress: 1 min → 2 min → with one arm raised (simulating signalling for help).
  • Distance swim — 50m (10 min): Students swim 50m continuously using any stroke. Focus on relaxed, sustainable effort. Record time; this is their baseline.
  • Survival breaststroke / sidestroke (10 min): Teach the energy-efficient survival strokes — head above water, slow and steady. When and why you'd use these instead of freestyle.
❄️ DEBRIEF 10 min
  • "Which stroke used the least energy? Which would you use if you were swimming for your life — not racing?" Link to rip current scenario from the classroom unit.
  • Complete Session 2 checklist rows.
S3

Throw Rescue, Clothing Float & Signalling

⏱ 50 min🏊 Pool + Deck Skills: throw rescue, clothed float, signal
🔥 ENGAGE 5 min

Throw Rescue Connection

  • Refer to the throw rescue ranking activity from the classroom unit: "Today we actually DO the throw rescue. You've ranked the equipment — now let's use it."
🏊 SKILL PRACTICE 35 min
  • Throw rescue (15 min): Pairs — one in water (treading), one on deck. Practise throwing a rope so it lands just past the swimmer. Pull in. Swap. Discuss: why throw PAST the person? What happens if you throw short?
  • Signalling for help (5 min): In water: practise raising ONE arm above the water while treading with the other. Teacher explains: this is the universal drowning signal. Practice matters because it's hard to do when panicked.
  • Clothed survival float (15 min): Students enter the pool in their school uniform (t-shirt + shorts/pants — pre-arranged). Experience the difference clothes make to movement and buoyancy. Float on back for 30+ seconds. This is a powerful, memorable activity.
❄️ DEBRIEF 10 min
  • "How did clothes change how you moved? What surprised you? Why do most drowning incidents happen in everyday clothes?"
  • Complete Session 3 checklist rows.
S4

Assessment Session — Skills Demonstration

⏱ 50 min🏊 Pool Summative practical assessment
🔥 WARM-UP 8 min
  • Free practice time — students can warm up any skills they feel less confident about.
  • Brief reminder of assessment criteria using the Observation Rubric (tab 3).
🏊 ASSESSMENT CIRCUIT 35 min

Stations rotate. Teacher observes and records on the Observation Rubric. Students can be assessed individually or in pairs.

  • Station A — Entry & Float: Feet-first pool entry from standing. Immediately roll to survival back float for 60 seconds.
  • Station B — Treading water: Tread water for 2 minutes. Final 30 seconds with one arm raised (signalling position).
  • Station C — 50m distance swim: Continuous 50m. Note stroke used and estimated energy efficiency.
  • Station D — Throw rescue: From poolside, throw rope to a partner in water at 4m distance. Two attempts.
❄️ UNIT REFLECTION 7 min
  • Students complete the self-reflection section on their Skills Checklist.
  • Class discussion: "What is the one skill that could save your life? Why?"
  • Connect back to the DRS ABCD and SwimSafe rules from the classroom unit — the theory and the practice are now linked.

✅ Differentiation — Session 4

  • Non-swimmers / low confidence: Float assessment in shallow end; distance reduced to 25m or width; throw rescue from edge in kneeling position
  • High ability: Add a timed component; 100m distance swim; throw rescue with a moving target; survival sequence (entry → float → tread → signal → swim 25m)
